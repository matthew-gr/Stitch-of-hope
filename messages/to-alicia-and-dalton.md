**Subject:** Stitch of Hope — three ideas for after launch

Hi Alicia and Dalton,

The site is live and looking great. Whenever you're ready, there are three things worth considering as a next phase — all optional, all free or low-cost to start. I wanted to float them before moving further so you can steer.

**1. A monthly journal / blog**
Short posts — 3–4 a year minimum, ideally monthly — about the workshop, the women you're working with, a new fabric, a piece of Kigali life. Google loves this kind of content for small local businesses and it's one of the cheapest ways to grow organic traffic. It's also a great archive of the story as it builds. Low effort if we keep posts to 300–500 words with 2–3 photos.

**2. Payments on the site**
Right now the site is a "request and we'll respond" flow, which is intentional for handmade work. When you're ready to sell directly, we can add MTN Mobile Money, Airtel Money, and card checkout via Flutterwave or Paystack — both work well in Rwanda and are used widely across East Africa. We'd need to decide on pricing structure, shipping options, and inventory tracking first. Not urgent — only worth doing when demand is there.

**3. A simple newsletter**
One short email a month — new arrivals, a story from the workshop, an upcoming market or pop-up. Free to start with Mailchimp (up to 500 subscribers). The value isn't in the size of the list, it's in having a direct channel to the people who love what you make, outside of Instagram's algorithm.

None of these are urgent. Each is "do when the moment feels right." Happy to spec any of them in more detail, or park them for now.

Let me know which — if any — you'd like to dig into.
